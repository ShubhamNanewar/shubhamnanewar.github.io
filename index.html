Who I Am

Quant Finance, Trading & Research

I'm a quant professional and FRM Charterholder, currently pursuing an MSc in Quantitative Finance at Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam (Duisenberg Honours Programme). My academic focus spans stochastic processes, econometrics, derivatives pricing, algorithmic trading, and quantitative investing.

I'm drawn to problems at the intersection of mathematics and markets — whether that's pricing derivatives, building systematic strategies, or modelling risk. Active investing in markets isn't just a hobby; it's how I bridge theory with the real behaviour of financial instruments.

On the professional side, I've built quantitative risk models at Wells Fargo (Treasury IRRBB) and worked in margin, collateral, and financing at Arcesium (D.E. Shaw Group) — giving me hands-on exposure to how capital flows through the financial system.

Off the screen — lawn tennis, badminton, cooking, travelling, and staying close to markets. Originally from a small town in central India, now based in Amsterdam 🇳🇱

VU Amsterdam · Ongoing Thesis
Event Price Risk of Earnings Announcements in US Equity Options
Examining how earnings announcements drive discrete price risk in US equity options and how implied volatility behaviour around these events can be exploited for vega and gamma hedging strategies. Modelling the jump component via Merton Jump Diffusion — capturing discontinuous price moves at announcement as a Poisson arrival process layered on top of standard GBM.
